Complete Registration Process

📝

Step-by-Step Registration Guide

  1. Go to the Administration Building and show your admission letter. Your documents will be verified there and you'll get a slip for tuition fee payment at Finance Section Room No. 13.
    Note: Tuition fee payment timing is 10:00am – 12:00pm. ICCR scholars must only ask for receipt and contact ICCR for admission fees.
  2. Return to Room No. 20, show fee receipt, collect folios, fill them, and get them signed. Two folios will be collected there.
  3. Apply for hostel at Dean of Students (DOS). Girls applying for Yamuna Hostel (single rooms) must write to VC; others can directly apply. Hostel allotment takes 3–5 days.
  4. Go to your School/Center, choose courses, and get signatures from Administrative Officer (A.O.) and your supervisor/chairperson.
  5. Register at Library, submit documents, get your Library ID and Internet Access number.
  6. Collect University ID card from your school's A.O.
  7. Submit Add/Drop folios for optional courses within the prescribed deadline.

Registration Requirements

📋

Required Documents

  1. Passport with Student/Research Visa
  2. Original Degree Certificate (with English Translation if needed)
  3. Letter from your home university/embassy
  4. Letter from scholarship institution (if applicable)
  5. Health Certificate (home country or Delhi hospital)
  6. 20 passport photographs
  7. Money for tuition fee and hostel registration
  8. Photocopies of all documents

Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO)

🏢

FRRO Registration

All foreign nationals must register at FRRO Delhi within 14 days of arrival (after registering at JNU). Documents required include:

  1. Bonafide certificate from JNU
  2. University ID number
  3. Residence proof
  4. Passport and visa
  5. Admission copy
  6. Photocopies of all documents with photos
  7. FRRO form (available on FRRO website)

Academic Resources

🏛️

Central Library

The heart of JNU's academic infrastructure. Register after admission to access an extensive collection of books, journals, and digital resources. The Central Library provides comprehensive research materials across all disciplines and offers various study spaces for individual and group work.

  • Vast collection of books and journals
  • Digital resources and online databases
  • Inter-library loan services
  • Research support services
📚

School/Centre Libraries

Specialized libraries within each School and Centre offer subject-specific collections for advanced research. These libraries house extensive collections of books, journals, and research materials tailored to specific academic disciplines and research areas.

  • Subject-specific collections
  • Specialized journals and publications
  • Research materials for advanced studies
  • Faculty and researcher resources
📖

Reading Rooms

Quiet, dedicated study spaces available in every centre across the campus. These reading rooms provide peaceful environments for focused study, research, and academic work, ensuring students have access to conducive learning spaces whenever needed.

  • Quiet study environments
  • Available in every centre
  • Individual study spaces
  • Extended hours during exams
